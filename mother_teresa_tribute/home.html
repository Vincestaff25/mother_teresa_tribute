Although Teresa enjoyed teaching at the school, she was increasingly disturbed by the poverty surrounding her in Calcutta. A famine in 1943 brought misery and death to the city; and the outbreak of Hindu/Muslim violence in August 1946 plunged the city into despair and horror.
  • Jan 2, 1948: Missionary Work
  • Oct 7, 1950: Teresa Begins Servig the Destitute.
  • Apr 23, 1952: Home for the Dying.
  • Apr 19, 1955: Mother Teresa soon opened a home for those suffering from Hansen's disease, commonly known as leprosy, and called the hospice Shanti Nagar (City of Peace).
  • May 31, 1982: In 1982, at the height of the Siege of Beirut, Mother Teresa rescued 37 children trapped in a front line hospital by brokering a temporary cease-fire between the Israeli army and Palestinian guerrillas.
  • Feb 20, 1991: In 1991, after a battle with pneumonia while in Mexico, she suffered further heart problems.
  • Sep 19, 1997: On March 13, 1997, she stepped down from the head of Missionaries of Charity and died on September 5, 1997, nine days after her 87th birthday.
